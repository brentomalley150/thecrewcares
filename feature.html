New feature · Proposal

Meet Kira — your crew's AI care coordinator

A warm, knowledgeable assistant on the home screen. Type what the crew needs in plain English — Kira creates the task, schedules the event, and lets the right person know.

Natural language Creates tasks & events Announced in crew chat
The short version

Instead of navigating menus to add a task, invite someone, or check the calendar, crew members just type what they want. Kira reads the intent, proposes the action, and posts a note to chat so the whole crew stays in sync. It's one input field that replaces a dozen interactions.

Why this feature

Our competitive analysis surfaced a clear insight: Caring Village shipped an AI assistant called Julia in late 2025, and it's already moving AI from "nice differentiator" to "table stakes" in family caregiving. But Julia is built as a separate assistant tab — ask it a question, get an answer. Kira is different.

1

It's embedded, not separate

Kira lives on the home screen as the primary input, not hidden behind a tab. It becomes the default way to do anything in the app.

2

It does things, not just answers them

Julia gives advice. Kira creates tasks, schedules events, and posts to crew chat. The difference between a help desk and a colleague.

3

It's visible to the whole crew

When Kira does something, it posts a short note in crew chat. No surveillance, no surprise — everyone sees what the AI did and why.

How it works

A concrete walkthrough. Brent opens CrewCares, sees the Kira card on the home screen, and types:

1

The user types a natural-language intent

Plain English works. Specific details work. Vague works too — Kira will ask for clarification if it needs to.

"Remind Sarah to pick up Dad's prescription tomorrow"
2

Kira proposes actions before taking them

Critical design choice: Kira always shows what it plans to do first. No surprise tasks appearing. Review, edit, then confirm.

Got it. Here's what I'll set up:
✓ Pick up Dad's prescription — Assigned to Sarah · Tomorrow
✓ Let Sarah know — Small note in crew chat
3

On confirm, actions execute simultaneously

The task appears in Sarah's list. A note appears in crew chat. The home screen updates. All visible to the whole crew.

In crew chat: "Kira added 'Pick up prescription' for Sarah based on Brent's note."
4

Kira items are clearly labeled

Every task, event, or chat message Kira created carries a small visible tag. Trust is built on transparency — the crew can always see what came from a human vs. the AI.

What else Kira handles

V1 supports three intent types — each solves a real friction in the current flow.

Create tasks

Parses assignee, due date, and action from a sentence. Handles "remind," "tell," "ask," "need to," and similar phrasings. Defaults gracefully when something's missing (unassigned task, "this week" due date).

"Tell Michael to schedule the PT appointment by Friday"

Schedule events

Recognizes time expressions ("Thursday at 2pm," "tomorrow morning," "next week"). Extracts event type from context (appointment, visit, meeting). Adds to the shared calendar.

"Dad has a cardiology follow-up Thursday at 10:30am"

Weekly summaries

A crew member who's been away can type "catch me up" or "what's happening this week" and get a concise summary of completed tasks, active work, medication changes, and upcoming events. No separate dashboard to check.

"Catch me up on this week"

V1 scope vs. later

Clear boundaries for the first release. Everything in "later" is on the roadmap but deliberately held back.

V1 ships with

Core three capabilities

  • Create tasks from natural language
  • Schedule calendar events from natural language
  • Generate weekly summaries on demand
  • Post announcements to crew chat when actions happen
  • "Confirm before action" pattern for all creations
  • Visible Kira tag on all AI-created items
Later releases

Deeper capabilities

  • Proactive suggestions from crew chat patterns
  • "Who should do this?" recommendations based on crew history
  • Medication reminder setup from natural language
  • Document summarization (discharge instructions, care plans)
  • Voice input on mobile
  • Cross-crew insights for users managing multiple care situations

Risks we've thought about

Kira is the right feature, but there are real pitfalls. These are the ones we're designing around.

Demo magic vs. real-world reliability

AI that "just works" in a prototype is easy. Reliably parsing "remind Michael to bring the oxygen tank when he visits on Sunday" every time is hard. Strategy: start narrow (three intent types), design for graceful failure, lean on the confirm step.

Trust when Kira gets it wrong

The AI will occasionally misassign or misdate something. Our approach: low-friction undo, clear "Kira made this" tags, and never post to chat without a confirm step. Mistakes should feel normal, not alarming.

Privacy and scope boundaries

Kira reads crew chat to generate summaries. We'll ship a clear toggle ("Let Kira read crew messages") and default to opt-in. The user controls what Kira sees.

Avoiding feature creep in the pitch

"AI that does everything" oversells. We're specific: v1 creates tasks, schedules events, summarizes the week. That's it. Vision talk about patterns and proactive suggestions is clearly flagged as later.

The investor framing

Three reasons Kira strengthens the CrewCares investor story.

It's our answer to Julia

"We have AI too" is table stakes. "Our AI is the primary input, not a help desk" is a position. Kira reframes the AI conversation instead of losing it.

It reinforces our wedge

We compete on chat-first, family-first simplicity. Kira is an agentic extension of chat — not a clinical records feature. It makes the existing positioning stronger, not muddier.

It unlocks the "one surface" narrative

Instead of shipping medications, journaling, and documents as separate features, we ship one agent that can orchestrate anything. One surface, infinite capability. Better product story, better engineering leverage.
